How is public transport in Ginnheim? 

 

Like most German cities, public transport is the easiest way to get around town. Besides regional and interstate trains there are busses, subways and trams which bring people to work or tourists around town. If you want to plan your trip around Frankfurt I would advise you look up the public transport system RMV. 

In Ginnheim most people live, but work further downtown.  So they rely a lot on public transport. One downpoint to oldtown Ginnheim is that you have to walk around 10 minutes to the next tram station or you take a bus. 

If you walk, you get rewarded by beautiful old houses and parks.      

So, even though Ginnheim is not the predestined place to stay as a tourist, I would encourage you to come here - especially when you get exhausted by the trouble in downtown. You don't need a car, but maybe a bike and some planning in advance.
